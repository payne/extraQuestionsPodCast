Ham Radio Extra Class Question Pool
Section E1 - 68 Questions
============================================================

E1A01
Q: Why is it not legal to transmit a 3 kHz bandwidth USB signal with a carrier frequency of 14.348 MHz?
A: (D) The upper 1 kHz of the signal is outside the 20-meter band
Ref: [97.305, 97.307(b)]

E1A02
Q: When using a transceiver that displays the carrier frequency of phone signals, which of the following displayed frequencies represents the lowest frequency at which a properly adjusted LSB emission will be totally within the band?
A: (D) 3 kHz above the lower band edge
Ref: [97.301, 97.305]

E1A03
Q: What is the highest legal carrier frequency on the 20-meter band for transmitting a 2.8 kHz wide USB data signal?
A: (C) 14.1472 MHz
Ref: [97.305, 97.307(b)]

E1A04
Q: May an Extra class operator answer the CQ of a station on 3.601 MHz LSB phone?
A: (C) No, the sideband components will extend beyond the edge of the phone band segment
Ref: [97.301, 97.305]

E1A05
Q: Who must be in physical control of the station apparatus of an amateur station aboard any vessel or craft that is documented or registered in the United States?
A: (C) Any person holding an FCC issued amateur license or who is authorized for alien reciprocal operation
Ref: [97.5]

E1A06
Q: What is the required transmit frequency of a CW signal for channelized 60 meter operation?
A: (B) At the center frequency of the channel
Ref: [97.303(h)(1)]

E1A07
Q: What is the maximum power permitted on the 2200-meter band?
A: (C) 1 watt EIRP (equivalent isotropic radiated power)
Ref: [97.313(k)]

E1A08
Q: If a station in a message forwarding system inadvertently forwards a message that is in violation of FCC rules, who is primarily accountable for the rules violation?
A: (B) The control operator of the originating station
Ref: [97.219]

E1A09
Q: Except in some parts of Alaska, what is the maximum power permitted on the 630-meter band?
A: (D) 5 watts EIRP (equivalent isotropic radiated power)
Ref: [97.313(l)]

E1A10
Q: If an amateur station is installed aboard a ship or aircraft, what condition must be met before the station is operated?
A: (A) Its operation must be approved by the master of the ship or the pilot in command of the aircraft
Ref: [97.11]

E1A11
Q: What licensing is required when operating an amateur station aboard a US-registered vessel in international waters?
A: (B) Any FCC-issued amateur license
Ref: [97.5]

E1B01
Q: Which of the following constitutes a spurious emission?
A: (D) An emission outside the signal’s necessary bandwidth that can be reduced or eliminated without affecting the information transmitted
Ref: [97.3]

E1B02
Q: Which of the following is an acceptable bandwidth for digital voice or slow-scan TV transmissions made on the HF amateur bands?
A: (A) 3 kHz
Ref: [97.307(f)(2)]

E1B03
Q: Within what distance must an amateur station protect an FCC monitoring facility from harmful interference?
A: (A) 1 mile
Ref: [97.13]

E1B04
Q: What must the control operator of a repeater operating in the 70-centimeter band do if a radiolocation system experiences interference from that repeater?
A: (C) Cease operation or make changes to the repeater that mitigate the interference
Ref: [97.303(b)]

E1B05
Q: What is the National Radio Quiet Zone?
A: (C) An area surrounding the National Radio Astronomy Observatory
Ref: [97.3]

E1B06
Q: Which of the following additional rules apply if you are erecting an amateur station antenna structure at a site at or near a public use airport?
A: (A) You may have to notify the Federal Aviation Administration and register it with the FCC as required by Part 17 of the FCC rules
Ref: [97.15]

E1B07
Q: To what type of regulations does PRB-1 apply?
A: (C) State and local zoning
Ref: [97.15]

E1B08
Q: What limitations may the FCC place on an amateur station if its signal causes interference to domestic broadcast reception, assuming that the receivers involved are of good engineering design?
A: (D) The amateur station must avoid transmitting during certain hours on frequencies that cause the interference
Ref: [97.121]

E1B09
Q: Which amateur stations may be operated under RACES rules?
A: (C) Any FCC-licensed amateur station certified by the responsible civil defense organization for the area served
Ref: [97.407]

E1B10
Q: What frequencies are authorized to an amateur station operating under RACES rules?
A: (A) All amateur service frequencies authorized to the control operator
Ref: [97.407]

E1B11
Q: What does PRB-1 require of state and local regulations affecting amateur radio antenna size and structures?
A: (B) Reasonable accommodations of amateur radio must be made
Ref: [97.15]

E1C01
Q: What is the maximum bandwidth for a data emission on 60 meters?
A: (D) 2.8 kHz
Ref: [97.303]

E1C02
Q: Which of the following apply to communications transmitted to amateur stations in foreign countries?
A: (C) Communications must be limited to those incidental to the purpose of the amateur service and remarks of a personal nature
Ref: [97.117]

E1C03
Q: How long must an operator wait after filing a notification with the Utilities Technology Council (UTC) before operating on the 2200-meter or 630-meter band?
A: (B) Operators may operate after 30 days, providing they have not been told that their station is within 1 kilometer of PLC systems using those frequencies
Ref: [97.303(g)]

E1C04
Q: What is an IARP?
A: (A) A permit that allows US amateurs to operate in certain countries of the Americas

E1C05
Q: Under what situation may a station transmit third party communications while being automatically controlled?
A: (B) Only when transmitting RTTY or data emissions
Ref: [97.221(c)(1), 97.115(c)]

E1C06
Q: Which of the following is required in order to operate in accordance with CEPT rules in foreign countries where permitted?
A: (C) You must have a copy of FCC Public Notice DA 16-1048

E1C07
Q: What notifications must be given before transmitting on the 630- or 2200-meter bands?
A: (D) Operators must inform the Utilities Technology Council (UTC) of their call sign and coordinates of the station
Ref: [97.303(g)]

E1C08
Q: What is the maximum permissible duration of a remotely controlled station’s transmissions if its control link malfunctions?
A: (B) 3 minutes
Ref: [97.213]

E1C09
Q: What is the highest modulation index permitted at the highest modulation frequency for angle modulation below 29.0 MHz?
A: (B) 1.0
Ref: [97.307]

E1C10
Q: What is the maximum mean power level for a spurious emission below 30 MHz with respect to the fundamental emission?
A: (A) - 43 dB
Ref: [97.307]

E1C11
Q: Which of the following operating arrangements allows an FCC-licensed US citizen to operate in many European countries, and amateurs from many European countries to operate in the US?
A: (A) CEPT
Ref: [97.5]

E1C12
Q: In what portion of the 630-meter band are phone emissions permitted?
A: (D) The entire band
Ref: [97.305(c)]

E1D01
Q: What is the definition of telemetry?
A: (A) One-way transmission of measurements at a distance from the measuring instrument
Ref: [97.3]

E1D02
Q: Which of the following may transmit encrypted messages?
A: (B) Telecommand signals from a space telecommand station
Ref: [97.211(b)]

E1D03
Q: What is a space telecommand station?
A: (B) An amateur station that transmits communications to initiate, modify, or terminate functions of a space station
Ref: [97.3(a)(45)]

E1D04
Q: Which of the following is required in the identification transmissions from a balloon-borne telemetry station?
A: (A) Call sign
Ref: [97.119(a)]

E1D05
Q: What must be posted at the location of a station being operated by telecommand on or within 50 kilometers of the Earth’s surface?
A: (D) All these choices are correct
Ref: [97.213(d)]

E1D06
Q: What is the maximum permitted transmitter output power when operating a model craft by telecommand?
A: (A) 1 watt
Ref: [97.215(c)]

E1D07
Q: Which of the following HF amateur bands include allocations for space stations?
A: (A) 40 meters, 20 meters, 15 meters, and 10 meters
Ref: [97.207]

E1D08
Q: Which VHF amateur bands have frequencies authorized for space stations?
A: (D) 2 meters
Ref: [97.207]

E1D09
Q: Which UHF amateur bands have frequencies authorized for space stations?
A: (B) 70 centimeters and 13 centimeters
Ref: [97.207]

E1D10
Q: Which amateur stations are eligible to be telecommand stations of space stations, subject to the privileges of the class of operator license held by the control operator of the station?
A: (B) Any amateur station so designated by the space station licensee

E1D11
Q: Which amateur stations are eligible to operate as Earth stations?
A: (D) Any amateur station, subject to the privileges of the class of operator license held by the control operator
Ref: [97.209]

E1D12
Q: Which of the following amateur stations may transmit one-way communications?
A: (A) A space station, beacon station, or telecommand station
Ref: [97.207(e), 97.203(g)]

E1E01
Q: For which types of out-of-pocket expenses do the Part 97 rules state that VEs and VECs may be reimbursed?

Ref: [97.527]

E1E02
Q: Who is tasked by Part 97 with maintaining the pools of questions for all US amateur license examinations?
A: (C) The VECs
Ref: [97.523]

E1E03
Q: What is a Volunteer Examiner Coordinator?
A: (C) An organization that has entered into an agreement with the FCC to coordinate, prepare, and administer amateur operator license examinations
Ref: [97.521]

E1E04
Q: What is required to be accredited as a Volunteer Examiner?
A: (D) A VEC must confirm that the VE applicant meets FCC requirements to serve as an examiner
Ref: [97.509, 97.525]

E1E05
Q: What must the VE team do with the application form if the examinee does not pass the exam?
A: (B) Return the application document to the examinee
Ref: [97.509(j)]

E1E06
Q: Who is responsible for the proper conduct and necessary supervision during an amateur operator license examination session?
A: (C) Each administering VE
Ref: [97.509]

E1E07
Q: What should a VE do if a candidate fails to comply with the examiner’s instructions during an amateur operator license examination?
A: (B) Immediately terminate the candidate’s examination
Ref: [97.509, 97.511]

E1E08
Q: To which of the following examinees may a VE not administer an examination?
A: (C) Relatives of the VE as listed in the FCC rules
Ref: [97.509]

E1E09
Q: What may be the penalty for a VE who fraudulently administers or certifies an examination?
A: (A) Revocation of the VE’s amateur station license grant and the suspension of the VE’s amateur operator license grant
Ref: [97.509]

E1E10
Q: What must the administering VEs do after the administration of a successful examination for an amateur operator license?
A: (C) They must submit the application document to the coordinating VEC according to the coordinating VEC instructions
Ref: [97.509(m)]

E1E11
Q: What must the VE team do if an examinee scores a passing grade on all examination elements needed for an upgrade or new license?
A: (B) Three VEs must certify that the examinee is qualified for the license grant and that they have complied with the administering VE requirements
Ref: [97.509(i)]
