Ham Radio Extra Class Question Pool
Section E3 - 39 Questions
============================================================

E3A01
Q: What is the approximate maximum separation measured along the surface of the Earth between two stations communicating by EME?
A: (D) 12,000 miles, if the moon is “visible” by both stations

E3A02
Q: What characterizes libration fading of an EME signal?
A: (B) A fluttery, irregular fading

E3A03
Q: When scheduling EME contacts, which of these conditions will generally result in the least path loss?
A: (A) When the Moon is at perigee

E3A04
Q: In what direction does an electromagnetic wave travel?
A: (D) It travels at a right angle to the electric and magnetic fields

E3A05
Q: How are the component fields of an electromagnetic wave oriented?
A: (C) They are at right angles

E3A06
Q: What should be done to continue a long-distance contact when the MUF for that path decreases due to darkness?
A: (B) Switch to a lower frequency HF band

E3A07
Q: Atmospheric ducts capable of propagating microwave signals often form over what geographic feature?
A: (C) Large bodies of water

E3A08
Q: When a meteor strikes the Earth’s atmosphere, a linear ionized region is formed at what region of the ionosphere?
A: (A) The E region

E3A09
Q: Which of the following frequency ranges is most suited for meteor-scatter communications?
A: (C) 28 MHz - 148 MHz

E3A10
Q: What determines the speed of electromagnetic waves through a medium?
A: (D) The index of refraction

E3A11
Q: What is a typical range for tropospheric duct propagation of microwave signals?
A: (B) 100 miles to 300 miles

E3A12
Q: What is most likely to result in auroral propagation?
A: (C) Severe geomagnetic storms

E3A13
Q: Which of these emission modes is best for auroral propagation?
A: (A) CW

E3A14
Q: What are circularly polarized electromagnetic waves?
A: (B) Waves with rotating electric and magnetic fields

E3B01
Q: Where is transequatorial propagation (TEP) most likely to occur?
A: (A) Between points separated by 2,000 miles to 3,000 miles over a path perpendicular to the geomagnetic equator

E3B02
Q: What is the approximate maximum range for signals using transequatorial propagation?
A: (C) 5,000 miles

E3B03
Q: At what time of day is transequatorial propagation most likely to occur?
A: (C) Afternoon or early evening

E3B04
Q: What are “extraordinary” and “ordinary” waves?
A: (B) Independently propagating, elliptically polarized waves created in the ionosphere

E3B05
Q: Which of the following paths is most likely to support long-distance propagation on 160 meters?
A: (D) A path entirely in darkness

E3B06
Q: On which of the following amateur bands is long-path propagation most frequent?
A: (B) 40 meters and 20 meters

E3B07
Q: What effect does lowering a signal’s transmitted elevation angle have on ionospheric HF skip propagation?
A: (C) The distance covered by each hop increases

E3B08
Q: How does the maximum range of ground-wave propagation change when the signal frequency is increased?
A: (C) It decreases

E3B09
Q: At what time of year is sporadic-E propagation most likely to occur?
A: (A) Around the solstices, especially the summer solstice

E3B10
Q: What is the effect of chordal-hop propagation?
A: (A) The signal experiences less loss compared to multi-hop propagation, which uses Earth as a reflector

E3B11
Q: At what time of day is sporadic-E propagation most likely to occur?
A: (D) Between sunrise and sunset

E3B12
Q: What is chordal-hop propagation?
A: (B) Successive ionospheric refractions without an intermediate reflection from the ground

E3B13
Q: What type of polarization is supported by ground-wave propagation?
A: (A) Vertical

E3C01
Q: What is the cause of short-term radio blackouts?
A: (D) Solar flares

E3C02
Q: What is indicated by a rising A-index or K-index?
A: (A) Increasing disturbance of the geomagnetic field

E3C03
Q: Which of the following signal paths is most likely to experience high levels of absorption when the A-index or K-index is elevated?
A: (B) Through the auroral oval

E3C04
Q: What does the value of Bz (B sub z) represent?
A: (C) North-south strength of the interplanetary magnetic field

E3C05
Q: What orientation of Bz (B sub z) increases the likelihood that charged particles from the Sun will cause disturbed conditions?
A: (A) Southward

E3C06
Q: How does the VHF/UHF radio horizon compare to the geographic horizon?
A: (A) It is approximately 15 percent farther

E3C07
Q: Which of the following indicates the greatest solar flare intensity?
A: (D) Class X

E3C08
Q: Which of the following is the space-weather term for an extreme geomagnetic storm?
A: (D) G5

E3C09
Q: What type of data is reported by amateur radio propagation reporting networks?
A: (D) Digital-mode and CW signals

E3C10
Q: What does the 304A solar parameter measure?
A: (B) UV emissions at 304 angstroms, correlated to the solar flux index

E3C11
Q: What does VOACAP software model?
A: (C) HF propagation

E3C12
Q: Which of the following is indicated by a sudden rise in radio background noise across a large portion of the HF spectrum?
A: (B) A coronal mass ejection impact or a solar flare has occurred
